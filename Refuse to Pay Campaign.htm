REFUSE TO PAY

 

Razors4U (R4U) is a start-up e-commerce company that sells popular brands of razor blades and shaving products to the UK and Europe.  R4U offers products as single purchases or through a subscription model, in which razor blades automatically arrive through a customer’s letterbox every 4, 8 or 12 weeks. Pretty simple.The Razors4U.com website went live in November 2014, selling top brand products up to 40% cheaper than major competitors (offline and online) along with free shipping. The concept is fantastic, however the challenge was getting customers excited about purchasing razor blades online. This is why R4U called Lab.

 

The key marketing challenge for Lab was to grow the customer base cost-effectively, whilst educating the market about the greater concept of buying razor blades online. Working with digital agency Lab and Kenshoo’s Infinity Suite, R4U was able to grow the average paid search conversion rate by 267% (cutting cost per acquisition in half), whilst simultaneously increase the customer base by 300%.

The remarkable conversion rates and customer base increase were due to a combination of strong strategic planning, innovative technology and pioneering techniques including the use of Neuro-Linguistic SearchTM , a methodology trademarked by Lab which combines Neuro-Linguistic Programming, psychology and emotional triggers in ad copy to transform user behaviour and disrupt traditional thinking. In essence, we use human psychology to make performance marketing incredibly effective.

 

 

The Objectives

­­­­

Razors4U’s primary marketing challenge was awareness, particularly due to the fact that shoppers were not used to buying razor blades online in the UK. Working with digital agency, Lab, R4U identified search as a critical channel to aid in acquisition, retention and growth as part of a broader digital marketing strategy that also included display, social, remarketing and email.

To help fulfill the brief, as Kenshoo's only UK black belt agency - Lab employed Kenshoo’s Infinity Suite to manage, track and optimize paid search. Initially, R4U ran small scale trials for traffic acquisition using search and social.

From this stage, the site was seeing an average conversion rate of 3% through paid search, which is in-line with typical online retail conversion rates of between 2% to 5%.

However, cost per acquisition (CPA) was very high – circa £12 to convert each customer! The product pricing strategy was so aggressive and working with such tight margins, that profitability would come from either increasing conversion rates or cutting CPA - ideally both

Specifically for search, the following campaign goals were set:

  • Grow the conversion rate by 50% in order to o achieve an average conversion rate of 4.5% (where a conversion is a purchase)
  • Reduce the CPA by 33% to £8 per customer
  • Grow the customer base by 70%

 

 

Strategy & Insight

 

R4U needed to grow its customer base cost-effectively and educate the market on its proposition. The opportunity was large – essentially targeting any man or woman that shaved, but as it involved changing ingrained user behaviour (buying blades without reference to price at the supermarket/pharmacy), the campaign required consumers to transform how they shopped. As a start-up, another challenge facing R4U was that branded search terms around Razors4U and variants would not yield high search volume as nobody was aware that the brand even existed. It therefore needed to be innovative, set clear objectives and use search to aid acquisition, retention and growth as part of a broader digital marketing strategy that also included display, social, remarketing and email. 

Success, particularly around cost-effectively attracting and converting customers was crucial to R4U’s success.

Innovation

How was this campaign innovative?

 

On the face of it, R4U could simply have relied on delivering a well-planned search campaign built on best practice in terms of campaign structure. It could also have just relied on the best technology (Kenshoo) to optimize campaigns over time, boosting budgets towards top performing keywords and ad groups. What made this campaign stand out and smash industry benchmarks for conversions and CPA, is the use of Neuro Linguistic Programming (NLP) in tandem with performance marketing insights.

It would have been easy to shoot for industry average performanc, but Lab’s expertise in Neuroscience had a staggering effect on campaign performance that is rarely seen as a point of differentiation with other agencies. In fact, the method was so successful that we trademarked it!

Smart Execution

 How did we do it?

Lab worked with R4U on building an effective campaign structure with well researched keywords and ad groups around products and branded terms.  But the team knew this would not ‘cut it’ on its own.  Before spending a single penny, it was critical to understand user behaviour.

Targeting was very broad:

Men and women in the UK who don’t want hairy faces or legs. Location aside, for text search, demographics are irrelevant as you can only bid on what people are actually searching for.

Broadly, most people buy blades habitually, as they fill shopping trollies/baskets in supermarkets or pharmacies. Most will admit they blanch at the cost of replacement blades, but couldn’t repeat how much they actually cost. Therefore, whilst price was key to the positioning, the strategy had to take in that consumers may not even realise that R4U’s pricing is hyper competitive.

To take account of this and to use price and other benefits effectively within search ad copy, customers were segmented using the psychology of priming and motives – specifically, the Monkey, Lion and Dog model (Lab’s own psychological model). At a high level, this recognizes that humans are driven by context, logical brain and emotions. So the prime motives for all search campaigns were based around the following:

  • Lions (Literal/Logical Brains) – I am looking to purchase blades at the best price and will search by brand
  • Monkeys (Contextual) – I’m on a health or beauty website where encountering an ad is in context
  • Dogs – I’m surfing the internet, socializing, reading content but not looking for anything in particular and an ad disrupts what I am doing

Underlying the campaign structure and Lab’s effective use of ad copy, Kenshoo’s Infinity Suite was configured to work in real-time, constantly analysing campaign performance and optimizing budget across the campaign. 

 

Effectiveness

 

Top line performance against campaign objectives - May ‘15 to Sep ‘15:

  • Average conversion rate across the five month campaign rose to a remarkable 8% (267% increase) – well above industry average
  • CPA reduced by 50% to £6
  • Customer base increased by 300% - subscription orders more than doubled!

The real value for the business was layering psychology and linguistic science in both search copy (display and semantic search) and creative aspects (retargeted display ads) to what would otherwise have been ‘just’ a good solid search campaign for a commodity purchase.

The aim was to disrupt online user behaviour and wherever possible, fire an emotional trigger, particularly to the right, non-conscious decision making side of the brain  – regardless of whether searchers are monkeys, lions or dogs!

One example perfectly illustrates the power of this approach in the construction of simple three line search ads. Ads for Mach 3 razor blades highlighting the price, delivered disappointing performance, possibly because people may remember their negative response to the high price in the supermarket, but can’t recall the actual price. So if most people see an ad with ‘£9.45 for 8 blades’, they don’t know if it’s a good deal or not.

Lab split tested various executions, including ‘30% off RRP’ and ‘30% cheaper than the high-street’.

Eventually it tested an advert with the words “Refuse to pay £15 for 8 blades”, believing it fired an emotional response around the high price, and communicated the value offered by R4U. Although a subtle change, in the first 24 hours, the ad performed better (based on clicks) than all other ads combined at a ratio of 15:1!  While the ad most appeals to logical Lions, the emotional ‘refuse to pay’ terminology works across the board (retargeted banner ads were designed to strongly appeal to monkeys and dogs). 

By surpassing expectations and using search to aid acquisition, retention and growth as part of a broader digital marketing strategy, this developed into a true digital transformation story for a David V Goliath start-up taking on the largest online retailers and pharmacies. Performance exceeded all expectations and secured additional funding for the marketing budget.
